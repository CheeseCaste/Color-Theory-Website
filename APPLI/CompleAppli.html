How to color with complementary colors

Hereâ€™s a step-by-step guide using Purple as the base color and Yellow as the complementary color:

Step 1: Base Color - Purple

Start with Purple as the primary color. Use this for the sky, mountains, and background. It should dominate most of the scene.
Purple in the sky: This sets a calm and mystical atmosphere. Make the sky a gradient of light to darker shades of purple to create depth.
Mountains and ground: Use varying shades of purple to give a layered effect, showing different mountain ranges. The deeper the shade, the farther the mountains appear.

Step 2: Complementary Color Yellow for the Sun

Add the sun using Yellow. Place it in the sky so that it contrasts strongly against the purple background.
Make the sun the focal point: The bright yellow draws attention and adds warmth to the scene. This contrast of yellow against purple highlights the importance of the sun in the composition.

Step 3: Balance of Colors

Maintain harmony by keeping the focus on the purple elements. Only use yellow in small areas, like the sun or highlights, to avoid overwhelming the design.
The yellow sun stands out but does not overpower the overall calmness created by the purple, preserving balance.

Step 4: Foreground Details

Tree and shadows: Add a tree or other elements in darker shades of purple to ground the composition. This helps connect the different elements of the picture without introducing too many distracting colors.

Step 5: Contrast and Focus

Teach contrast: The strong contrast between purple and yellow demonstrates how complementary colors create emphasis. This can help in understanding how to direct focus to key elements (like the sun) while maintaining an overall sense of harmony.

Step 6: Tasks for students

Students can experiment with their own versions by applying complementary colors (like purple and yellow) to highlight focal points in artwork. They can try enhancing different elements without overwhelming the design, learning how to effectively use contrast and balance.

The use of purple and yellow in this scene illustrates the power of complementary colors. Purple creates a calm, cool environment, while yellow adds warmth and contrast, ensuring the design remains visually engaging and balanced.

This method can help teach students about effective color use in design, ensuring they understand how to create a visually appealing and balanced composition.
