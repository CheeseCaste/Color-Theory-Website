How to color with triadic colors

Here’s a step-by-step guide using blue, violet and green for analogous colors:

Step 1

First, start to define your background and use a simple yet light color for the background. Warmer colors can make the artwork have more sense of space and an atmosphere.

Step 2

Add orange to some of the lighter green areas.

Step 3

Some of the darker blues are pushed towards a violet.

Step 4

Lastly, to make the distant shore deep, add dull red. Adding some colors brought the blues and greens to life.

I like to think of analogous colors as cousins. . . they’re close to each other and have some blood in common, but they’re in different families. - Mark Nesmith
