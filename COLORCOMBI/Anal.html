Lesson 6: Analogous colors

Pick a color

An analogous color scheme refers to colors that are adjacent to each other on the color wheel. This type of combination is commonly used to create designs with smooth transitions and harmonious blending, as the colors share a similar base hue, which leads to a naturally pleasing visual experience. The smooth gradation of analogous colors is useful when teaching students how to maintain color harmony in their artwork or designs.

In this example, the colors are blue, blue-green and yellow-green Blue and yellow-green are directly adjacent to green, creating a seamless flow. Yellow green extends the combination slightly into a warm territory but remains within the harmonious range.

This color combination teaches students about maintaining visual unity in their work, which is particularly useful in branding, landscape art, and designs where a cohesive aesthetic is crucial.
