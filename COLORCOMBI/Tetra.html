LESSON 8: Tetradic colors

Pick a color

Tetradic color scheme consists of 4 colors which have 2 sets of complementary color. The 4 colors can be obtain by forming a rectangular or square shape in the color wheel. An example of tetradic color scheme is red, light blue, green and purple.

This is an example by Barbara Sailor, using blue-violet, red-orange, yellow-orange, and blue green tetradic colors. This perfectly balanced each color, not one dominating the other that it would be a pain to look at but colored in a way where the subject you would have to look at are balanced from each other. The brightness of the red-orange makes it eye-catching as well as its complement, blue-violet and yellow orange tone down its intensity and allow as well the viewer's eye to move around the piece not just in one place.
