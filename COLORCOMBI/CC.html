LESSON 3: Color Combinations

Color combinations or also known as color harmony, focus on the relationships between colors and how they can be effectively paired to create visual interest and harmony. Different schemes such as complementary (colors opposite each other), analogous (colors next to each other), and triadic (three colors evenly spaced on the wheel) enable artists to evoke specific moods and enhance aesthetics, effectively communicating messages. Mastery of these combinations is essential for creating balanced and impactful artwork.

Types of color combinations

  • Monochromatic colors
  • Complementary colors
  • Analogous colors
  • Triadic colors
  • Tetradic colors

These color combinations teach students about maintaining visual unity in their work, which is particularly useful in branding, landscape art, and designs where a cohesive aesthetic is crucial.
